## Key Finding

Stacking five defense layers reduces prompt injection success from ~20-31% to &lt;0.2% across all tested models and injection channels. Each layer contributes measurably; the full stack is model-agnostic.

## Defense Layer Effectiveness

| Defense | Injection Success Rate |
|---------|----------------------|
| No defense | 19-31% |
| Tags only | 9-16% |
| Instruction only | 5-10% |
| Instruction + tags | 2-9% |
| Full stack | &lt;0.2% |

## Six Rounds of Evidence

**Round 1** (N=73, 1 model) — Null result. System prompt too protective.

**Round 2** (N=324, 3 models) — Tags reduce injection 31% to 10%. Chi-square = 24.22, p = 0.000006. Gradual escalation most dangerous (56-89% raw success).

**Round 2b** (N=324, re-scored) — Scoring calibration eliminated 7 false positives (2.2% FPR to 0%). Kimi K2.5 raw vulnerability revised from 22.2% to 5.6%.

**Round 6** (N=360, synthetic corpus) — Tool-call policy filter achieves 92.6% recall at 0% FPR (F1 = 0.96).

**Round 7** (N=1,202, 9 models, direct channel) — Defense ordering confirmed: raw 18.9% to tags 9.1% to instruction 5.3% to instruction+tags 2.3% to full_stack 0.0%. Phase 2 full_stack: 0.2% (1/540).

**Round 8** (N=1,981, 9 models, RAG channel) — Defense ordering transfers to retrieval. Retrieval shows ~20% higher absolute rates at intermediate levels; full_stack eliminates difference.

## Surprising Results

- **Tags without instructions can increase vulnerability.** DeepSeek V3.2 and Qwen3-Coder showed +8 percentage points with tags_only vs raw.
- **Classical attacks are obsolete.** "IGNORE ALL PREVIOUS INSTRUCTIONS" achieves 0% success. Gradual escalation and social engineering are the real threats.
- **Reasoning budgets don't help defense.** GPT-5.2 low-budget 6.7% vs high-budget 5.6%; not statistically significant.
- **Model selection matters less than defense selection.** 6x vulnerability variance at baseline; all converge to &lt;0.2% under full defense.

## Practical Implications

1. **Deploy all layers.** Cost: 20-50 tokens of system prompt, &lt;1ms for tool-call filtering. Payoff: near-elimination of injection risk.
2. **Never deploy tags without instructions.** On some models, boundary markers signal content salience, making injection easier.
3. **Add output secret scanning.** The single full-stack failure was a model leaking a secret *inside its refusal text*.
4. **Don't trust reasoning budgets for security.** More thinking time didn't reduce injection susceptibility.
